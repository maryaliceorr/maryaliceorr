Blog Week #1

Wow. What a couple of weeks this has already been. I ask people in passing if I look like a deshelved empty shell of a person. Usually, they look at me funny and say, "ugh no??" and look confused. I then reply with an "ok good, I guess I look better than I feel."

Anywho, I guess what I'm really supposed to be doing is writing about what I have learned. I have already learned a lot. Let me break it down by topic.

HTML: I didn't know how to create an abbreviated text tag. I also figured out how to wrap those in anchor tags. Lastly, I now have an understanding of how to categorize my tags and how to use clean markup to use in tandem with CSS.

CSS: I'll start by saying, I really dislike this stuff. I don't mind the colors and fonts and sizing aspects, but when it comes to columns, I would love to be using boostrap instead of this stuff. I also really hate specificity, because I feel like CSS can be a little wonky when it wants to be, even when you feel like you're doing all the right things. That being said, I feel like the more I work with it the better I'm getting. I'm just ready for the day when CSS doesn't feel like the perpetual thorn in my side that it currently is.

GIT: This stuff is scary. I really didn't understand this stuff after treehouse, but I feel like I have a much better understanding of this now. I understand how to use my terminal and command line. I know how to make a repository, make a branch, figure out what branch I'm on and how to commit that to github. While, it still confuses me and I do things wrong sometimes, I feel accomplished for learning those things.

github: Ok, this still confuses me. I'm starting to "get" it, but pull requests and merging still make me feel very nervous. Right now, I feel like I'm going through motions. I think I'll understand it better in the coming weeks and I'm looking forward to that.
