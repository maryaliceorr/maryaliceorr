BIO

Who am I? My name is Mary Alice Orr and I am constantly evolving my career in technology. I suppose you could classify me as a "Jane of all trades" because I enjoy lots of things. What kinds of things you might ask? Well, when I'm working I enjoy creating, building, tinkering and analyzing anything and everything. In my free time I enjoy paddleboarding with my husky, rock climbing in the great outdoors, photographing national parks, playing jazz, or cooking a gourmet meal. So...how did I get to where I am now?

As a child, I was fascinated by how things worked. I wanted to know why they worked the way they did and what made them tick. I wanted to create, experiment, analyze, and improve anything and everything. Whether it was testing cookie recipes in my Easy-Bake Oven, conducting science experiments with household items or building toy robots, I wanted to understand how ideas became final products. My parents always thought I would become an engineer and I guess they were somewhat right.

For the last 6 years, I’ve specialized in digital advertising operations/marketing and along the way, something sparked a realization that I was missing an integral piece to my career path puzzle. At a previous position, I was part of a team that uncovered some malicious activity that was executed through code. I felt a responsibility to prevent such events from occurring to my employers in the future and it ignited a new hunger in me to explore code. That desire led me to Nashville Software School to attend their Web Development Jumpstart, and 3 months later, their 12-month Web Development Bootcamp. I’ve learned so much from going back to school, both inside the classroom and out. I love programming for both the challenge and intrinsic accomplishment I feel when I solve a problem. I’m excited to take all of the skill sets I’ve gained throughout my career and utilize them to my fullest potential as I grow my career in tech.

Bottom line, I will always seek to learn, to critically think about my work, to evolve, to overachieve, to grow and I'm still striving to understand what makes things tick.
